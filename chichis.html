Chichi's

The goal of this project was to develop a food delivery application for Chichi’s --a Latin American restaurant based in Ann Arbor, Michigan-- in a cross-functional way. All areas of the UX pipeline were covered --user research, back-end programming and front-end programming.

Researching the job

When I was first approached by Chichi’s founder, I was asked to make an application similar to Doordash or UberEats. Chichi’s was in need of an online platform where they could handle their delivery process. The reason for this change was the increasing demand and popularity of Chichi’s on campus. Customers loved the product, but they were not very happy with the delivery process as most of the interactions were happening through multiple applications.

As a firm believer in the Jobs To Be Done methodology and rapid iteration, I proposed that we first interviewed customers to discover what was causing them to order Chichi’s products.

Research questions

  • What do Chichi’s customers hire its products for?

  • What are the related social, emotional and functional jobs?

  • What are their desired outcome statements?

  • How do they measure success?

After conducting 20 interviews and analyzing the data I uncovered two main functional jobs and 10 desired outcome statements (how customers measure success).
