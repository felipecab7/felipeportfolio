About the project

Designing a habit tracker
The Sumi prototype was the culmination of a semester-long project for SI 482 - Interaction Design Studio at the University of Michigan. We learned to define and narrow the scope of a problem, analyze the competitive landscape, empathize with users, ideate solutions and test them.
Role
UX researcher
Interaction designer
Visual Designer


The challenge
One of the requirements of the first stage of this project was to find a problem that adhered to the CHI 2019 student competition’s main theme: Weaving the Threads within the Social Fabric. We were encouraged to consider the ways that technology might be used to strengthen our social fabric --how individuals interact with each other within a community. We choose to tackle the problem many people face when making new year's resolutions: following through with those promises. We believed one of the reasons this happens is the lack of an accountability system where goals would be shared in a group setting and members would push each other to succeed.
Competitive Analysis
We kicked off the research phase by analyzing the competitive landscape of our problem. We identified four competitors.
Coach.me
It allows users to track habits and share them with friends or the community for accountability. Users can hire a personal certified coach who will guide them through meeting specific goals.
Find Your Ditto
An application that focuses on bringing people with chronic illnesses together. It allows users to find peers with similar experiences to chat with each other.
Facebook Messenger
We picked Facebook Messenger to be our analogous competitor because we were inspired by the community of content sharing that the application evokes.
HabitShare
It allows users to share their habits with friends for extra accountability and motivation. Its best features include being able to customize who sees each habit you are tracking and creating custom goals

Low-fidelity mockups
Paper prototype
After conducting the QOC analysis and drawing intial mockups of the screens, we proceeded to create paper prototypes with two tasks in mind: create a group and join a group. This process allowed us to test the interactions before transitioning into a digital protoype.


Observation 1
Users complained about not being able to add a category and a picture to each challenge.
Observation 2
Two users expressed the desire to have a more conventional dashboard where they could see their picture, select the challenges they belong to and create new challenges.
Observation 3
Users took more than 7 seconds deciding which option to choose from the pop-up window triggered after clicking on a challenge. Users expressed their desire to be directed to the challenge without having to deal with an extra menu.
High Fidelity Prototype
Our high fidelity prototype was the culmination of weeks spent ideating, desining and iterating. We transitioned to digital after learning through mockups, wireframes and paper prototypes what impact our ideas would have on users. Thanks to the nature of rapid iteration, many of the wrong assumptions we had were discarded and that helped us move forward towards functionalities that based on data made a positive impact on users.
