SI 206: Data-Oriented Programming Final Project

For my SI 206 final project, I was asked to gather data from APIs, store that data in a database, calculate new data from the database, create visualizations from the calculated data and print out a report with the calculated data and visualizations.

Goals:

  1. Create a fully-working program without any scaffolding.

  2. Create and modify tables in a SQLite Database.

  3. Utilize APIs (including researching possible methods).

  4. Utilize visualization software (including researching options).

Social Influence and Sentiment on Twitter

Multiple theories of social influence explain that our attitudes, feelings and beliefs are influenced by the groups we belong to. Since the emergence of social media, our ability to connect and belong to large groups has increased; raising questions about its impact on our happiness and mental health. Twitter is a great example of how we can easily create a network (large or small), share our thoughts and learn what the members of the network are thinking. For this project, I decided to explore the relation between the size of those networks (number of people users follow) and the emotional tone of their tweets.

I used a sample of 7355 tweets, gathered in the span of a week from 1000 cities across the U.S, from 4878 unique users. I stored the data in database tables using SQL and calculated it using a custom-built sentiment classifier. The classifier analyses a tweet’s emotional tone by counting the number of positive and negative words and labels it as either “Positive” or “Negative”.

Results

The first graph shows a negative correlation between the number of people users follow and the number of positive tweets they tweeted in the span of seven days


No clear pattern was found on the second graph due to the limited number of tweets labeled as negative



Given the subjective nature of the tweets, the method used in this project cannot be utilized alone to judge whether a tweet should be labeled as having a positive or a negative emotional tone. The results above show a csorrelation between the number of people a user follows and the amount of positive words they include in their tweets regardless of what the emotional tone of the tweet is. The more people users follow, the less positive words they used in the span of seven days.
