My purpose

It was the day of the show. Minutes before going on stage I heard my name. My excitement had turned into terror, my heart kept beating faster every second and my hands would not stop sweating. A couple of months prior, I had been given the opportunity to sing at my brothers prom, in front of two hundred people. I was fifteen and had never played in front of of an audience besides small family gatherings, so you can imagine how I was feeling.

Once I got to the door behind stage, I was given a microphone. But something was wrong — it was a wireless microphone, unlike the one we had been using in rehearsal. As if I wasn't already nervous that my voice would be heard by two hundred people, I knew that If I didn't move around stage I would look awkward, lost and nervous. My mom was in the front row and the last thing I wanted was to disappoint her.

I finally got on stage, lights went on, and I started singing. I sounded a little nervous at first, but as time passed, I became more comfortable. I focused so much on the song that I forgot the audience was there. It was as if stepping on stage brought out an inner self alive with energy and confidence. It felt amazing. Despite my far from perfect performance, it felt right to be in front of an audience and see their emotional response.

I wasn't the best at singing, but fortunately I knew how to playing the piano. This pushed me to continue learning how to read music and convey emotions through songs. I then picked up other interests in filmmaking, painting and graphic design. After analyzing multiple career paths, I realized the underlying reason I loved making songs, paintings, and short-films was the story structure behind them. It was fascinating to see how a well-crafted story can impact somebody's life: How an animated movie can me adults cry, how a song can make an audience sit on the edge of their seats and how a great story can make designers engage in a more personal way with users. There is no doubt this is the reason I loved performing from the beginning. All those family gatherings were the best channel for me to inspire people through storytelling.

My increased interest in graphic design and storytelling led me to apply to the School of Information at the University of Michigan where I earned a degree in User Experience Design. I learned how to better listen to users, understand the story behind their struggles and engage with teammates and stakeholders in discussions grounded in those root needs. I learned that —just like in a movie —users see themselves as the hero in their own lives on a journey, with a set of obstacles, wanting to make progress and wanting to hire products that help them overcome those struggles. As a UX designer, that is what I strive to do. I want to make sure those struggling moments drive the product development process so that the solutions I create can change and inspire people.
